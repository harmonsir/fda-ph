FDA Advisory No.2024-1658 || Reminders on Collectible Toys

Share this Post!

Toy collectibles are increasingly popular in recent times. These miniature replicas of iconic characters, vehicles, and objects can be valuable investments and provide entertainment. These entice both children and adults, despite being marketed to the latter. Although adults are the target market for these products, children may foreseeably play with them. Toy collectibles bear attractive features, color and marketing that may attract children to play with them.  However, without the appropriate precautions, this seemingly harmless pastime may raise potential hazards when inadvertently placed in the hands of the wrong consumer.

One of the primary concerns with toy collectibles is the risk of choking children. The increased hand-to-mouth activity of small children coupled with the small size and presence of small parts may lead to the unintended swallowing of these small items. For this reason, the FDA provides the following general safety recommendations in the handling of toy collectibles:

  1. Avoid giving toy collectibles with small, detachable parts to children under 3 years old, as they pose a choking hazard. As a guide, the FDA has developed an anti-choking tester, “Choke Guard” to guide consumers in checking small parts that could pose as a choking hazard. Please visit https://bit.ly/ChokeGuard.
  2. Ensure that items are made of non-toxic, lead-free, and phthalate-free materials. Look for these especially for plastic, paint, or other finishes on items.
  3. Check the label of toy collectibles for the recommended age group and instructions for use if they are not suitable for young children.
  4. Store toy collectibles properly a secure location, out of reach of young children.

To report any safety incidents involving collectibles, please send an e-mail to info@fda.gov.ph or call our hotline at (02) 8857-1900 local 8113 or 8110.
